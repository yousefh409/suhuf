## One-liner

The student of knowledge’s AI companion: it listens while you read, structures what you learn, and uses AI to make sure you never forget it

## Core Features

- Live Arabic Readalong → Read any Arabic Islamic text aloud and get real-time feedback on grammar or morphology mistakes. Easily view transalation and i3rab information for any word with a simple tap. The platform follows along in the digitized text, catches errors as they happen, and shows why the word takes that form.
- Structured Note-Taking → Problem: If you use Notion or Google Docs, your notes live in a separate app with no connection to the book. If you write in physical margins, those notes can't be searched, reviewed, or shared. Need to be creative in how we do this → integrations with Notion, iPad companion app, how does user take notes, etc
- AI-Powered Review → Goes beyond flashcards, and will be customized and personalized by AI. Duolingo type reviews daily with multiple question modalities. Users can request a prompt-driven review and let AI create reviews for them based off what they want to learn. Teach-back session: User can explain a concept in voice (teaching is best way of learning)
- **Additional Features to consider:**
    - Integrate lectrures and sharhs about books on-platform → lots of youtube series and so on easily available. Can also lead to “originals” produced specifically for our platform
    - Memorization review → For students memorizing text a dedicated memorization review mode (similar to tarteel)
    - In-Depth Sentence & Textual analys → don’t just give word meaning and morphology info but go further: its place in the sentence, root words, etc (similar to the Kalam quran app)
    - Hadith visualizer → let users tap on a hadith to view isnaad, source, other narrations, etc
    - Knowledge pathways → structured course(s) for specific sciences (with accompanying youtube lectures)

## Competitive Landscape

- Tarteel
    - Focused on Quran memorization
- Niyyah App
    - Note made for classes but for more surface-level users
- RemNote ($2M ARR, 700k users)
    - Note-taking app with built-in flashcard creation and spaced repetition. Any bullet point becomes a flashcard. PDF annotation with card generation from highlights.
    - Review is still flashcard-only, no multi-modal review, no reading eKalaamxperience, etc
- Kalaam
    - Quranic Arabic vocabulary learning app. Teaches the 92 grammar patterns covering 95% of Quranic Arabic. Uses spaced repetition for vocabulary. Good for Quran-specific vocabulary but doesn't extend to broader Islamic texts.
- Ascii ($11M ARR, 2M users)
    - Spaced repition with card decks + open-source customization
    - No note-taking, no AI, and UI/customization limited to technical people
- Readwise ($8M ARR, 100-200k users)
    - Captures highlights from Kindle, Apple Books, Instapaper, Pocket, PDFs, and web articles, then resurfaces them via "Daily Review”
- StudyFetch, Knowt, Quizlet, NeuraCache
    - Upload-and-study AI tools. All optimized for cramming (usage spikes during finals), not deep mastery. No reading experience and no persistent knowledge building
- SeekersGuidance, AlMaghrib, Bayyinah, etc
    - Online course platforms with live/recorded classes
